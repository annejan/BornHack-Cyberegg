Weight & Type 2 Diabetes

shipped

A sixth pet stat, weight, tracked the same way as Hunger/Tired/Drained/Sick/Miserable — 0 is best, full-scale is worst. Shown as the Fit bar in the Stats popover.

How it moves

Weight drifts up on its own, slowly — a multi-day arc rather than an hours-scale stat like Hunger. Left completely alone it crosses the overweight line in roughly 4–5 days. Feeding adds a further nudge on top, sized entirely by what was eaten. Exercising is the only way to bring it back down.

The diabetes trigger

weight > 60% → sustained 3 days (Classic) / 6 days (Casual) → diabetic = true, permanently

Dropping weight back under the line before that window elapses resets the clock to zero — no partial credit. Once triggered, though, there's no cure, only management: while diabetic and unmedicated, Sick accrues an extra penalty every tick, on top of whatever else is already pushing it up.

Announcing it

The moment diabetes triggers, the badge plays a buzzer alert and takes over the entire screen for at least 3 seconds — "TYPE 2 DIABETES / Give medication soon" — before returning to normal. From then on, whenever medication has lapsed, a persistent NEEDS MEDS banner sits in the corner of the pet screen — the pet's own animation keeps playing underneath it, the banner doesn't take over the display. Earlier neither of these existed: the state silently swapped the whole pet display for plain debug text with no sprite art behind it (so the pet vanished) and nothing alerted the player at all. Fixed by pulling diabetic-unmedicated (and the still-artless weight critical/warning tiers) out of the sprite-selection ladder entirely and rendering the alert and banner as independent overlays instead.

Insulin & Ozempic

shipped

Both live inside the existing Heal icon, alongside "Give medicine" — the same pattern Rest already uses for Sleep + Relax under one icon. No new icon slot needed for either. ("Give medication" was renamed to Insulin for clarity now that there are two medications on the same menu.)

Insulin

Only selectable once the pet is diabetic; shows as a permanently disabled row before that, same treatment as "Defrost" sitting disabled outside Hibernate. Administering it resets a cooldown that doubles as a protection window: while it's running, the diabetes sick-penalty is suppressed. When it lapses, the penalty resumes right as the item becomes selectable again — the game's way of telling you it's time to re-dose.

Ozempic

A separate, stronger weight-loss treatment — not gated on being diabetic, unlike Insulin. Roughly double Exercise's weight relief in one dose, with a bit of appetite suppression (small hunger relief) and mild side-effect nausea (small drained cost) for flavor. Balanced by a cooldown far longer than Exercise's — a once-in-a-while option, not a routine substitute for actually exercising.

Drinks & Alcoholism

shipped

A second, parallel "permanent condition" track, same shape as weight/diabetes: new Drink icon (top row, 4th slot) offers five choices, each scaling baseline drunk-gain/drained-relief/weight-gain numbers by a percentage — Beer sits at 100% as the reference point.

DrinkDrunk gainDrained reliefWeight gainRead
Water0%100%0%no alcohol at all
Cola0%150%80%no alcohol, but sugary
Beer100%120%100%baseline
Wine150%110%70%more drunk than Beer, less weight
Whiskey250%140%30%most drunk by far, least weight — spirits are lower-calorie than beer

Unlike weight, drunk passively sobers up on its own over several hours — no Exercise-equivalent action needed. That makes the permanent condition genuinely about repeated heavy drinking: sustained drunk-above-trigger for a multi-day window (mirroring DIABETES_ONSET_TICKS) flips alcoholic permanently, same buzzer/full-screen-alert treatment, same "extra sick penalty until treated" consequence. Managed via Rehab, a fourth item under Heal alongside Give medicine/Insulin/Ozempic — gated on being alcoholic, same protection-window cooldown pattern as Insulin.

Drink and Rehab reuse the existing Feeding and Medicating animations respectively rather than getting dedicated sprite art — both are thematically "consuming something" / "receiving treatment" moments, and reusing already-cataloged anim IDs sidesteps the catalog-bounds bug found and fixed earlier this session.

Friends (SHDW mesh channel)

shipped

Every badge now auto-joins a private mesh channel called SHDW on boot — same deterministic key-derivation the built-in #bornhack/#test channels use, so every badge gets there with zero setup. While a pet is active, the badge broadcasts a small presence beacon on it every 15 minutes; when another SHDW badge is in LoRa range, the pets "meet".

EventEffect
New friendfirst time meeting that badge — a bigger one-time happiness boost
Reunionknown friend, cooldown (4h) elapsed — a smaller "spending time together" boost
Seen too soonknown friend, still on cooldown — no boost, just noted

The beacon is sent as a GrpData packet with a private data_type, not GrpTxt — it never shows up in the channel-browser chat UI or gets forwarded to the companion app, it's picked up directly by the game layer. Friend records (up to 20) persist in their own KV namespace, independent of the main save.

Check who's been met via Stats > Friends — now a proper clickable menu rather than a passive list. Each friend is one cursor-selectable row (word-wrapped so long name/kind combos never run off the 152px screen — the original single-line "name (kind) - since" layout could overflow), ordered most-recently-seen first: a friend jumps to the top of the list the instant a fresh beacon from them arrives. Fire on a friend opens a detail screen: kind, how long you've known them, how long since their last beacon ("Last seen"), their cached combat-stat snapshot, and your head-to-head Battle record against them specifically (see Battles). Any button on the detail screen steps back to the list; any non-navigation button on the list closes the whole screen.

Verification note — unit-tested in isolation (friend list add/dedupe/ring-overflow, beacon round-trip, cooldown boundary, head-to-head record update, most-recently-seen reordering) and built clean across all firmware variants including the standalone mesh-only and game-only builds. Actually meeting a friend requires two physical badges, both on this firmware, both in LoRa range — not something verifiable single-handed.
mesh

Battles with friends

shipped

A new Battle item — now the first option under the Play icon — lets you fight any pet on your Friends list. Every pet has four combat stats derived automatically from its existing care stats and traits, no separate grinding required.

Combat statDriven mainly by
AttackCuriosity trait + how well cared-for the pet is
DefenseResilience trait + how lean/fit the pet is
SpeedHow lean/fit the pet is + Vitality trait
HPVitality trait, reduced if the pet is sick

Being diabetic or alcoholic takes a flat penalty off Attack/Defense/Speed — the same permanent conditions that hurt everyday care make the pet a weaker fighter too.

Battles are instant, using cached stats — no waiting on the other badge. The periodic Friends beacon (every 15 min) now also carries the sender's current combat-stat snapshot, cached in the receiver's Friends list. Challenging a friend resolves the whole fight immediately, locally, using your pet's live stats plus that cached snapshot: a small deterministic turn simulation (both sides act every round, faster one first, seeded 80-120% damage variance, hard-capped at 20 rounds) decides the winner on the spot — no live round-trip over LoRa needed.

After resolving, the challenger broadcasts a small result packet on SHDW (same private GrpData pattern as the Friends beacon, distinct data_type) so the friend's badge can learn about it whenever it next receives it — updating its own win/loss tally (inverted: your win is their loss) and showing its own toast, even if they weren't looking at the Battle screen.

Battle HP is completely separate from the pet's real health — computed fresh at the start of every fight from current stats and discarded afterward. It never touches sick, the Leaving/Gone lifecycle, or anything else about the pet's actual wellbeing; losing a battle costs nothing but bragging rights.

Win/loss is tracked two ways: a lifetime counter on the pet overall (shown on the battle result screen and in Stats > Health status), and — new — a per-friend head-to-head record, stored on the FriendRecord itself and shown on that friend's detail screen (Stats > Friends, click a friend). Both sides of a battle update the same head-to-head tally: the challenger bumps it locally right after resolving, and the target bumps its own copy (inverted) upon receiving the result broadcast — so the record reads consistently whichever badge you check it from, not just a one-sided log. Battling has its own cooldown (120 seconds), same shape as every other action.

Verification note — unit-tested in isolation (combat-stat derivation bounds/penalties, battle-simulation determinism and guaranteed termination, result-packet round-trip, extended beacon/friend-record round-trip incl. the head-to-head fields, save-format round-trip) and built clean across all firmware variants including the standalone mesh-only and game-only builds. As with Friends, verifying the *other* badge actually receives the async result broadcast needs two physical badges in range — not verifiable single-handed.

Changelog

  • 2026-07-16 (12) — Friends list now shows "Last seen" per friend and sorts most-recently-seen first: `FriendRecord` gained a `last_seen_tick` field (distinct from `last_boost_tick`, which only moves on a cooldown-gated happiness boost) that updates on every beacon, and `FriendsList` moves the matching entry to the front of the ring whenever it updates. Detail screen layout tightened (14px bar spacing, Battle record + a "Back" hint sharing the bottom row) to fit the new line without crowding the 152px screen.
  • 2026-07-16 (11) — Fixed a real bug: Battle (and Friends) win/loss counts could inflate and disagree between badges, because the mesh's GrpData payload type — unlike GrpTxt chat messages — had no duplicate-suppression. A single broadcast reaching a badge via more than one mesh path (direct + a repeater hop, say) got processed once per path, each time re-applying a friend-boost or re-incrementing a win/loss tally. Added the same content-hash dedup ring GrpTxt already used (via relay_hash on the raw pre-decrypt payload, since GrpData has no timestamp to hash) ahead of both the beacon and Battle-result handlers. Also added defensive guards against a badge processing its own broadcast echo, and hardened the Battle screen to re-check the cooldown itself rather than relying solely on the Play menu's gate. Battle's cooldown shortened from 30 minutes to 120 seconds per user request.
  • 2026-07-16 (10) — Friends screen reworked into an actual clickable menu (word-wrapped rows, cursor + Fire to open a per-friend detail screen) instead of a passive multi-line list that could overflow the 152px screen.
